Protocol Overview

This integrative protocol is designed for a person with Rheumatoid Arthritis (RA) currently managed with biologic DMARD therapy. The goal is a two-pronged approach:

Short-Term Goal

Rapidly reduce active inflammation, joint pain, and flare frequency

Long-Term Goal

Support immune recalibration, joint tissue repair, and reduce biologic dependency over time

The protocol layers peptide therapy on top of existing biologic medication, focusing on complementary — not competing — mechanisms of action. Safety and biologic compatibility have been factored into every compound selected.

Phase 1 — Peptide Stack (Months 1–6)

All peptides are administered subcutaneously (SQ). Rotate injection sites to avoid localised reactions.

1. KPV
Primary Anti-Inflammatory & Immune Modulator
Derived from alpha-MSH. Inhibits NF-κB signalling, directly reducing TNF-α, IL-1β, and IL-6 — the exact same cytokines driving RA. Uniquely modulates immune response WITHOUT broadly suppressing immunity (critical while on biologics). Demonstrated reduction in pannus formation and osteoclast activity in arthritis models.
Dose500–1000 mcg SQ daily
TimingMorning, fasted
CycleDaily — no cycling required
Biologic Compatibility✓ Safe — different mechanism, no immunosuppression overlap
2. BPC-157
Joint Tissue Repair & Anti-Inflammatory
Promotes angiogenesis and new blood vessel formation to damaged joint tissue. Stimulates fibroblast activity and collagen synthesis in tendons and cartilage. Activates M2 macrophage polarisation (anti-inflammatory, pro-repair). Shown to reduce inflammation in autoimmune arthritis models. Protects against NSAID-induced gut damage.
Dose250–500 mcg SQ daily
TimingFlexible — morning or evening
Injection SiteInject near affected joints where possible for localised benefit
Biologic Compatibility✓ Safe — tissue repair pathway, no immune suppression interaction
3. Thymosin Alpha-1 (Tα1)
Immune Recalibration
The long-game peptide for RA. Tα1 doesn't just suppress — it retrains the immune system toward balance. Enhances regulatory T cell (Treg) function — the cells responsible for preventing self-attack. Promotes Th1 immune balance and reduces pathological Th17 activity (Th17 excess drives RA). Reduces infection risk — critical when on immunosuppressive biologics.
Dose1.5 mg SQ twice weekly
TimingMonday and Thursday (or any 2 days, 72hrs apart)
Cycle3 months on, 1 month off
Biologic Note⚠ DISCUSS WITH DOCTOR — Tα1 is immunomodulatory; monitor for any infection signs
4. GHK-Cu
Anti-Inflammatory & Joint Support
Inhibits NF-κB (same pathway as KPV — additive effect). Stimulates collagen and glycosaminoglycan synthesis — key components of cartilage. Reduces oxidative stress and cellular senescence in joint tissue. Antifibrotic: reduces excessive scar tissue formation in synovial membranes.
Dose1–2 mg SQ daily
TimingFlexible
CycleCan run continuously
Biologic Compatibility✓ Safe — no known interactions
5. NAD+
Cellular Energy & Immune Regulation
NAD+ levels are specifically depleted in Rheumatoid Arthritis — restoring them is foundational. Regulates T cell differentiation, reducing autoimmune CD4+ T cell aggression. Activates sirtuins — anti-inflammatory and longevity-associated proteins. Supports mitochondrial function in immune cells, improving energy and reducing chronic fatigue.
Dose (SQ)100–250 mg SQ, 2–3x per week
Dose (IV)500–1000 mg IV monthly
AlternativeNMN 500–1000 mg oral daily (lower bioavailability but consistent)
Biologic Compatibility✓ Safe — natural coenzyme, no interaction risk

Nutrition Protocol

Diet is not optional support — it's active therapy. A consistently anti-inflammatory diet is a force multiplier for everything else in this protocol.

✓ Prioritise These Foods

  • Fatty fish (salmon, sardines, mackerel) — 3–4x per week
  • Extra virgin olive oil — daily
  • Colourful vegetables (broccoli, kale, spinach, capsicum)
  • Berries (blueberries, cherries) — anthocyanins reduce joint inflammation
  • Ginger and turmeric (with black pepper)
  • Bone broth — collagen precursors
  • Eggs and organ meats — NAD+ precursors (niacin) and cofactors

✗ Eliminate These

  • Ultra-processed foods, seed oils (canola, soy, sunflower)
  • Refined sugar and high-fructose corn syrup
  • Gluten (trial elimination for 8 weeks)
  • Alcohol — depletes NAD+ and disrupts gut barrier
  • Fast food and trans fats

Lifestyle Protocol

Exercise

  • Resistance training — 3x per week
  • Swimming or hydrotherapy — ideal during flares
  • Daily walking — 20–40 min
  • Yoga or Tai Chi — improves joint mobility
  • Avoid high-impact during active flares

Sleep

  • Target 8–9 hours nightly
  • Consistent sleep/wake time
  • No screens 60 min before bed
  • Room temperature 18–20°C
  • Magnesium glycinate 400 mg + glycine 3 g before bed

Stress

  • Daily breathwork (5 min minimum)
  • Cold shower 2–3 min — reduces systemic inflammation
  • Morning sunlight — regulates cortisol rhythm
  • Sauna 3–4x per week — reduces joint stiffness
